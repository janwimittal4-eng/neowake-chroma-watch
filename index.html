What It Is

The Chroma Watch is worn on the wrist like a regular watch. Instead of tracking activity, it houses laser diodes that emit four colors of light — blue, green, yellow, and red at fixed wavelengths. You start a session with a button press and adjust duration and intensity from the device.

How the Science Is Understood

Photobiomodulation (PBM) is a real, actively studied field looking at how red and near-infrared light interacts with cells. Some PBM research has reported effects on circulation, inflammation, and tissue repair in controlled settings.

What that research doesn't establish is that this specific consumer wristband produces the same effects at home. PBM outcomes depend heavily on wavelength, dose, and exposure time — variables that differ from a single wearable used casually.

neowake markets the Chroma Watch as a wellness device for energy, circulation, and general wellbeing. Its own materials acknowledge limited medical evidence specific to this device. Treat claims about conditions like pain, sleep, or tinnitus as anecdotal, not clinical findings about this product.

FAQ

What is the neowake Chroma Watch?

A wrist-worn device emitting four wavelengths of light — blue, green, yellow, and red — through laser diodes, marketed as a photobiomodulation-based wellness product.

No independent clinical trial of this specific device has been published. PBM as a field is studied, but this exact product's claims lack dedicated clinical evidence.

No. It's sold as a wellness product, not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
